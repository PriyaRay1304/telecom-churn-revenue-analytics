# Key Insights & Business Recommendations

---

## Summary of Findings

Several clear patterns emerged from the analysis:

- Month-to-month contracts exhibit significantly higher churn compared to longer-term contracts
- Early-tenure customers (0â€“1 year) account for the largest share of revenue churn
- Revenue churn is higher than customer churn, indicating loss of higher-value customers
- High-risk customers represent a smaller customer segment but drive a disproportionate share of revenue at risk

---

## Business Implications

These findings suggest that:
- Retention efforts should focus on **early customer lifecycle stages**
- Contract structure plays a critical role in churn prevention
- High-value customers require proactive engagement and incentives

---

## Recommended Actions

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

1. Introduce incentives for month-to-month customers to migrate to longer contracts
2. Strengthen onboarding and engagement for customers in their first year
3. Prioritize retention campaigns for high-risk, high-revenue customers
4. Monitor revenue churn alongside customer churn as a core KPI

---

## What Could Be Analyzed Next

Potential next steps include:
- Predictive churn modeling using machine learning
- Retention strategy simulation and ROI analysis
- Time-based churn trend analysis with longitudinal data

---

This analysis demonstrates how combining customer behavior, revenue impact, and risk segmentation leads to more effective churn management strategies.
